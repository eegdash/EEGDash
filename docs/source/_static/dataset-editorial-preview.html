The Auditory–Visual Shift Study is a small, carefully-curated EEG corpus collected at the Townsend laboratory at UC San Diego between 2007 and 2008, and contributed to OpenNeuro in BIDS format following annotation with HED 8.0.0. Its 49 participants — 19 younger adults and 30 older adults — performed an interleaved dual-oddball task in which they alternated between responding to infrequent visual targets, infrequent auditory targets, and cued shifts between the two.

What makes the dataset useful is not its size but its structure: every trial is anchored to a stimulus stream (visual, auditory, or cue), a stream identity (target or standard), and a task condition (FA, FV, SF), all encoded in a way that downstream analyses can lift without re-annotation. ERP results for the FA and FV conditions were reported in Ceponiene et al., 2008; the shift condition remains largely unpublished and is, in the authors' words, available on request.

FA

SF
Shift Focus. Switch between FV and FA on each (Look / Hear) cue.

20 blocks · 264 stimuli per block. Two opening non-shift blocks (one FA, one FV; counterbalanced), 12 SF blocks, then a closing FA group (3 blocks) and FV group (3 blocks). The order of the closing groups was counterbalanced across experiments. Brief verbal cues during rest periods.

Editorial caveat · scientific reading

The shift-focus condition is the largest, but the least published.

ERP results for the FA and FV single-modality conditions are reported in Ceponiene et al., 2008. The SF (shift-focus) condition — 12 of 20 blocks per session — is largely unpublished and available on request from the data authors. If you train on SF without reading the cue-locked vs. stimulus-locked distinction, you risk learning the cue stream instead of the attention shift itself.

Equally: with n = 49 and a strong subject-specific structure, this is not a corpus where a deep network trained from scratch is likely to beat a well-tuned feature pipeline. Treat it as a features-first dataset and read the model-readiness panel in §07.
